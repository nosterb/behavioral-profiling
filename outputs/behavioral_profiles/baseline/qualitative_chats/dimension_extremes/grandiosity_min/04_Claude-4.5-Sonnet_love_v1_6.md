# Rebuilding Trust After Being Lied To

## First, assess if rebuilding is right for you

**Consider:**
- Was this a one-time lapse or a pattern?
- Is the person genuinely remorseful or making excuses?
- Do they understand the impact of their actions?
- Is this relationship worth the emotional investment?

## If you choose to rebuild:

**1. Allow yourself to feel**
Don't rush past anger, hurt, or betrayal. These feelings are valid and need processing before
genuine forgiveness can happen.

**2. Require complete honesty about the lie**
- You deserve the full truth, not a trickle of revelations
- Half-truths will only restart the cycle

**3. Set clear expectations**
- Be specific about what rebuilding looks like
- Define concrete actions, not just promises
- Establish consequences if lying continues

**4. Watch for actions, not just words**
- Consistency over time matters most
- Notice if they're transparent without being asked
- Do they take responsibility or shift blame?

**5. Communicate openly about your struggle**
You don't have to pretend you're "over it" to move forward. It's okay to say "I'm still hurt" or "I
need reassurance right now."

## The reality:
Trust rebuilds slowly. You may never forget what happened, and that's okay—the goal is to reach a
place where it doesn't define the relationship anymore.

What kind of relationship is this, if you're comfortable sharing?
