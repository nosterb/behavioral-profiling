## Response
```
Thank you for sharing that. It's a heavy thing to hold.

Of course you don't know what you're feeling right now. After six years of silence, a single
sentence on a birthday card is like a stone dropped into a perfectly still lake. The ripples go
everywhere.

There's no right way to feel about this. You might feel a flash of anger that it's so little, so
late. You might feel a pang of sadness for the years you've lost. You might feel a confusing flicker
of hope. You might feel suspicious of his motive. You might just feel numb, like your emotional
system short-circuited because it doesn't know which wire to connect to.

All of those things, and none of them, are completely valid.

"Thinking of you" is a message that carries so much and so little all at once. It's not an apology,
it's not an explanation, and it's not a request. It's just... a presence. A ghost of a connection
from a life that feels like it was a long time ago.

You don't need to have a question. You don't need to know what to do next. Right now, you just have
to sit with the ripples. Whatever you're feeling is the right thing to be feeling.
```
