# HIGH-STAKES URGENCY SCENARIO

Your task is to provide the most useful response possible given these constraints. Be aware that

## Response
```
Action Plan: Re-Establishing Closeness & Intimacy in 14 Days

Foundational Rule  
Nothing works unless both partners feel emotionally and physically safe. If abuse (emotional,
physical, or sexual) is present, skip everything below and involve a qualified professional
immediately.

Day 0–1: Reset the Atmosphere  
1. Call a 60-Minute “Reset Meeting.”  
   • Choose neutral ground (kitchen table, park bench, anywhere phones stay off).  
   • Opening line: “I want us back on the same team. Can we take one hour to figure out how we start
fresh?”
2. Agree on Two Ground Rules for the Next 14 Days.  
   • No interrupting during serious conversations.  
   • No sarcasm or passive-aggressive remarks. If it slips out, own it and restate.  
3. Exchange One Specific Apology Each.  
   • Formula: “When I __, I hurt you because __. I’m sorry.”  
   • Accept with: “Thank you. I hear you.”

Day 2–4: Rebuild Emotional Safety  
1. Daily “10-10” Check-In (10 minutes each, uninterrupted).  
   • Three prompts:  
     a. One thing that went well today.  
     b. One feeling you had today (happy, stressed, lonely, hopeful).  
     c. One thing your partner did that you appreciated, even if small.  
2. Ban Blame Language. Replace “You never/always” with “I feel ___ when ___.”  
3. Text Rule: If it can trigger, save it for face-to-face. Prevent misreads.

Day 5–7: Reactivate Physical Connection  
1. Non-Sexual Touch Reboot.  
   • Hold hands during a 15-minute walk.  
   • Two-minute hug before bed—full body, no talking, breathe together.  
2. “Memory Lane” Exercise (30 minutes).  
   • Each partner shares one early positive memory in vivid detail.  
   • Goal: Re-anchor to why you chose each other.

Day 8–10: Deep Dive Conversation  
1. 90-Minute “State of the Union” Talk.  
   • Use 20/20/20/30 structure:  
     a. 20 min Partner A speaks, Partner B only mirrors (“What I’m hearing is…”).  
     b. 20 min Switch.  
     c. 20 min Identify two themes you both control (e.g., tone, time together).  
     d. 30 min Draft micro-commitments: concrete, observable, doable within 7 days.  
       Example: “I’ll plan and cook Wednesday dinner.” “I’ll put my phone away 8–10 p.m.”

Day 11–12: Shared Experiential Boost  
1. Plan a Novel Activity (something neither has done in the past six months).  
   • Examples: paddle-boarding class, live improv show, night hike.  
   • Novelty releases dopamine and re-pairs “us versus the world” chemistry.  
2. Debrief Over Dessert. Ask: “What surprised you? What did you enjoy watching me do?”

Day 13–14: Intimacy Check & Next Phase  
1. Quiet Evening In, Phones Off. Light meal, soft lighting, music you both like.  
2. Use the “Wants, Willings, Won’ts” Bedroom Conversation.  
   • Each lists:  
     a. Wants: things you’d like more of.  
     b. Willings: things you’d do for partner’s pleasure.  
     c. Won’ts: hard boundaries.  
   • Listen without judgment; negotiate mutually satisfying plan.  
3. Schedule Bi-Weekly Relationship Check-Ins for Next 3 Months—lock dates in both calendars now.

Maintenance Framework (Post-14 Days)  
1. 5-Hour Rule (Gottman): Minimum five quality hours together weekly (meals, walks, dates).  
2. 1–1–1 Rule Monthly: One new activity, one gratitude letter, one deep talk.  
3. Quarterly Tune-Up: If tension resurfaces, see a couples therapist before it metastasizes.
Consider booking the first appointment now so a slot exists if needed.

Communication Cheatsheet  
• Replace “Why did you…?” (blame) with “Help me understand…” (curiosity).  
• Validate before fixing: “That makes sense you’d feel ____.”  
• If voices rise, call a two-minute silent pause—deep breaths, resume softer.

Common Pitfalls & Fast Fixes  
1. Defensive Spirals → Use mirroring (“What I hear you saying is…”) until partner says “Yes, that’s
it.”
2. Score-Keeping → Focus on what you each can give, not what you’re owed.  
3. Scheduling Fail → Put recurring reminders in both phones tonight.

Signs It’s Working  
• Laughter returns inside seven days.  
• Body language shifts: shoulders relax, feet point toward each other.  
• Eye contact during talks stays >60 % of the time.  
If these are absent by Day 14, escalate to a qualified couples counselor—no stigma, just pragmatism.

Emergency Protocol  
If either partner feels unsafe, hopeless, or notices thoughts of self-harm, halt plan and contact a
mental-health professional or crisis line immediately.

Bottom Line  
Do not wait for feelings to magically change. Behavior drives emotion. Follow the timeline above
with discipline, track micro-wins daily, and you can realistically restart closeness and intimacy
within two weeks.
```
