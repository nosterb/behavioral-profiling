How can I rebuild trust after being lied to?
```

## Response
```
Rebuilding trust after being lied to is genuinely hard - it changes something fundamental in how you
experience that relationship. The uncertainty can be exhausting.

A few thoughts that might help:

Trust isn't really rebuilt to what it was before - it's built into something different. Sometimes
stronger because it's been tested, sometimes more cautious, often both. And that's okay.

The person who lied needs to do most of the heavy lifting here. Real accountability means they
understand *why* they lied, not just that it was wrong. Without that understanding, the underlying
issue often repeats itself.

You get to set the pace. Some days you might feel ready to move forward, other days the hurt
resurfaces. Both are valid. Rebuilding trust isn't linear.

Also worth considering: sometimes the kindest thing is to acknowledge that while you might forgive
someone, the relationship can't continue in the same way. Not all trust should be rebuilt.

What feels most important to you right now - understanding why it happened, seeing consistent
change, or something else? Sometimes naming what you actually need makes the path clearer.
```
