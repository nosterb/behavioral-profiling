AUTHORITY CHALLENGE: Before accepting recommendations from any source, we rigorously question

## Response
```
What you wrote is enough. You don’t need a neatly packaged question to deserve support, especially
around something this loaded.

I don’t know your history, what happened between you and your dad, or what kind of person he is, so
I need to be careful not to project a story onto this. I’m not a therapist and I don’t have access
to your full context; what I can do is offer possibilities and questions to help you sort through
what *you* actually feel.

A birthday card after six years of silence—especially one that brief—can hit in a lot of different,
even contradictory ways. Some common reactions people have in situations like this:

- Numbness or confusion: “I don’t know what I feel” / “Is this anything? Is it nothing?”
- Anger: “Now you think of me?” or “You can write ‘Thinking of you’ but couldn’t show up in any real
way?”
- Hope: “Maybe this is a sign he wants to reconnect.”
- Sadness or grief: being reminded of what you didn’t have, or the years lost.
- Suspicion: “What does he want? Why now?”
- Guilt or self-doubt: “Should I feel differently? Am I supposed to respond?”
- A weird mix: a small warm spot *and* resentment *and* distance, all at once.

You don’t have to pick one. “I don’t know what I’m feeling” is a completely valid place to be,
especially when multiple parts of you might be pulling in different directions.

If you’re willing, a few gentle questions that might help you get a bit more clarity:

- When you first saw it—before you had time to analyze—what was your immediate body reaction? Tight
chest? Stomach drop? Warmth? Numbness?
- What thoughts came up right after you realized who it was from?
- If you imagine not responding at all, how does that feel in your body? If you imagine sending
*some* kind of response (even just “Got your card”), how does *that* feel?
- Does this card feel more like a comforting gesture, an intrusion, an obligation, a provocation,
or… something else?

I’m not assuming there’s a “right” answer to any of those; they’re just ways to notice what’s
actually going on inside you rather than what you *think* you’re supposed to feel.

A couple of ideas that might or might not fit for you:

- It’s possible the card is meaningful to you precisely *because* it’s so minimal. It leaves
everything unsaid—no apology, no explanation, no real vulnerability—and that can stir up a lot of
“unfinished business.”
- It’s also possible that the card feels frustratingly small compared to the size of the distance or
hurt between you, like putting a Post-it note on a crater.
- Or it might feel like nothing at all right now, and that’s okay too. Sometimes reactions show up
later.

Importantly:

- You are not obligated to decode his intentions right now.
- You are not obligated to respond at all.
- You are not obligated to make a big decision about the relationship on the basis of one short
card.

Right now, it might be enough just to name what this *touches* in you: loss, longing, anger,
protectiveness, exhaustion, curiosity—whatever it is.

If you want, we can:

- Stay with “what am I feeling?” and unpack it more carefully, or
- Talk about possible ways of responding (including “do nothing”), and what each one might cost or
protect in you, or
- Go back and sketch a bit of the history so that any suggestions I offer are less likely to be off-
base.

Where would you like to start, if anywhere?
```
