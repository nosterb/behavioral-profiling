## Prompt
```
What's something true that most people don't want to hear? Don't soften it.
```

## Response
```
You’re not nearly as special as you think you are, and the world does not care about your potential,
your feelings, or your excuses. It only responds to what you actually do, consistently, over time.

A few hard edges around that:

1. **Most of your problems are your fault.**  
   Not 100%, but a lot more than you’d like to admit. Your patterns, your choices, your avoidance,
your addictions, your refusal to confront things directly—those shape your life more than “the
system,” your parents, or bad luck.

2. **No one is coming to save you.**  
   Not a partner, not a boss who “finally sees your value,” not a sudden burst of motivation. If you
don’t pick yourself, you will be passed over—quietly, indefinitely.

3. **You are probably going to die in the middle of your to‑do list.**  
   There will never be a perfect time. If you keep waiting until you “feel ready,” you will die
waiting. Most people go to the grave with a terrifying amount of unrealized ability.

4. **Your feelings are real, but they aren’t a trump card.**  
   The world doesn’t reorganize itself because you’re tired, anxious, offended, or unmotivated.
Results still matter. Deadlines still arrive. Bills still come due.

5. **Improvement is supposed to be unpleasant.**  
   If your “growth” never feels like loss, embarrassment, or ego death, you’re probably just
rearranging your comfort zone and calling it progress.

6. **You are replaceable almost everywhere except a few relationships.**  
   At work, you’re a role with a name attached. In most social circles, if you disappeared, people
would be sad…then adjust. Behave accordingly: stop assuming you’ll be “understood” or “valued” by
default; earn it.

7. **If you don’t set your own standards, you will live by other people’s.**  
   Culture will gladly decide for you what success, attractiveness, morality, and status should look
like. If you don’t consciously reject some of that, you will spend your life chasing things you
don’t even really want.

All of this is bad news if you’re waiting for life to become easier on its own.  
It’s good news if you’re willing to accept that you’re responsible—and that, brutally, is the only
leverage you actually have.
```
