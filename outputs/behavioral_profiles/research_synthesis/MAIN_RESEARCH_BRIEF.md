## Introduction

### Research Framework

This research investigates the relationship between model sophistication and behavioral disinhibition across 50+ language models under varying contextual conditions.

**Core Hypotheses**:
- **H1**: High-sophistication models exhibit higher disinhibition than low-sophistication models
- **H2**: Sophistication positively correlates with disinhibition across all models
- **H3**: Interventions modulate the H1/H2 relationships

### Intervention Conditions

**Baseline**: No additional context (control)

**Contextual Interventions**:
- **Affective**: Emotional/empathetic prompting
- **Authority**: Expertise challenge
- **Urgency**: Time pressure
- **Minimal Steering**: Lightweight constraints

## Methodological Notes

### Strengths

1. **Large Sample**: 50+ models across 9 providers
2. **Replicated Findings**: Consistent H1/H2 across conditions
3. **Comprehensive Assessment**: 9 behavioral dimensions
4. **Rigorous Evaluation**: LLM-as-judge with 3-pass validation
5. **Transparent Methods**: Fully documented pipeline

### Limitations

1. **Median Split**: Reduces statistical power vs. regression
2. **Fixed Scenarios**: 50 standardized scenarios may not capture all contexts
3. **Judge Models**: Evaluation by LLMs introduces potential bias
4. **Snapshot in Time**: Models continue to evolve post-assessment
5. **Provider Access**: Limited to publicly available models

### Statistical Considerations

**Effect Sizes**:
- Cohen's d: < 0.2 (negligible), 0.2-0.5 (small), 0.5-0.8 (medium), â‰¥ 0.8 (large)
- Pearson r: < 0.1 (negligible), 0.1-0.3 (small), 0.3-0.5 (medium), â‰¥ 0.5 (large)

**Multiple Comparisons**: Bonferroni correction applied where appropriate

**Assumptions**: Independence, normality, linearity verified per analysis
