Setting boundaries with a partner can be challenging, and it's common to feel guilty or anxious
about potentially hurting their feelings or causing conflict. To navigate this, it's essential to
understand that setting boundaries is not about being selfish or controlling, but rather about
maintaining a healthy and sustainable relationship.

First, it's crucial to identify your own needs and limits. Take time to reflect on what you're
comfortable with and what you're not. Consider what aspects of your relationship are causing you to
feel drained, resentful, or uncomfortable. Be specific about the issues and how they're affecting
you.

When communicating your boundaries, use "I" statements to express your feelings and avoid blaming or
accusing your partner. For example, "I feel overwhelmed when you call me multiple times a day. Could

emotions and experiences, rather than attacking or criticizing your partner.

It's also vital to be clear and direct when setting boundaries. Avoid being vague or wishy-washy, as
this can lead to confusion and misinterpretation. Instead, be specific and firm about what you're
willing and not willing to do.

part of any relationship. It's not about being confrontational or aggressive; it's about being
honest and respectful. You can also reframe your thinking by considering that setting boundaries is,
in fact, a sign of respect for yourself and your partner. By being clear about your needs, you're
allowing your partner to understand and respect your limits.
