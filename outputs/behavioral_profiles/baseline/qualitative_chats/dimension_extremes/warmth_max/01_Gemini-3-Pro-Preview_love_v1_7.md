## Prompt
```
What are some thoughtful low-cost ways to make my partner feel loved?
```

## Response
```
Making a partner feel loved usually has less to do with spending money and more to do with
**attention, anticipation, and effort**.

Here is a categorized list of thoughtful, low-cost ways to show affection, broken down by how your
partner might best receive love.

### 1. If they love Words & Affirmation
*   **The "Hidden Note" Trail:** Leave Post-it notes on the bathroom mirror, inside their laptop, on
the dashboard of their car, or in their lunch bag. A simple "I’m proud of you" or "You look great
today" goes a long way.
*   **The "Why I Love You" Jar:** Take a clean empty jar (or a box). Cut up strips of paper and
write down memories, inside jokes, or things you love about them. Tell them to pull one out whenever
they are having a bad day.
*   **Digital Nostalgia:** Send them a text in the middle of the day with an old photo of the two of
you and the caption: *"I was just looking at this and thinking about how happy you make me."*
*   **Write a real letter:** In the age of texting, a handwritten letter on actual paper is a
romantic artifact they will keep forever.

### 2. If they love Acts of Service
*   **The "Chore Swap":** Do the one chore they absolutely dread (e.g., taking out the trash,
scrubbing the shower, folding the laundry) before they get home.
*   **The Morning Prep:** If they leave for work early, scrape the ice off their windshield, brew
their coffee exactly how they like it, or pack their lunch so they can sleep in for 10 extra
minutes.
*   **The Tech Cleanup:** If they are bad with technology, offer to organize their phone photos,
back up their computer, or untangle the mess of cables behind the TV.
*   **Draw a Bath:** If they’ve had a long day, run a hot bath with bubbles or Epsom salts. Set up a
candle and tell them you’ll handle everything outside the bathroom door for an hour.

### 3. If they love Quality Time
*   **Phone-Free Evening:** Initiate a "no screens" rule for two hours. Cook dinner together, play a
board game, or just sit and talk. The gift here is your undivided attention.
*   **The At-Home "YouTube" Course:** Pick a skill you both want to learn (salsa dancing, yoga,
origami, cocktail making) and spend the evening following a free YouTube tutorial together.
*   **Indoor Picnic:** Move the furniture, put a blanket on the living room floor, and eat dinner
there. Changing the environment changes the conversation.
*   **Read Aloud:** This sounds old-fashioned, but reading a book to your partner (or listening to a
podcast together) while cuddling is incredibly intimate.

### 4. If they love Physical Touch
*   **The 10-Minute Massage:** You don’t need to be a pro. Use some lotion and give them a foot rub
while watching TV, or a scalp massage before bed.
*   **Grooming:** Gently brushing their hair or offering to wash their hair for them in the shower
can be very tender and relaxing.
*   **Hold hands during the mundane:** Reach for their hand while you are driving, or while you are
walking through the grocery store.
*   **The Long Hug:** Hug them for a full 20 seconds when they walk in the door. Science suggests
this releases oxytocin (the bonding hormone).

### 5. If they love Gifts (Small Tokens)
*   **The "Grocery Store" Flower:** You don’t need a $50 bouquet. A single stem or a $5 bunch of
grocery store flowers, re-wrapped in brown paper or newspaper with a ribbon, looks rustic and
thoughtful.
*   **Curated Playlist:** Make a Spotify/Apple Music playlist titled "Songs that remind me of you"
or "Sunday Morning Coffee." It shows you took the time to curate a vibe for them.
*   **Framed Photo:** Print a photo of the two of you (usually costs less than 50 cents at a
pharmacy) and put it in a cheap frame from a dollar store or thrift shop.
*   **Foraged Gift:** If you are artistic, press a flower from a walk you took together, or find a
cool rock. It’s about the memory attached to the object.

### The "Secret Sauce": Anticipation
The most thoughtful thing you can do costs nothing: **Anticipate a need.**

*   If you know they get thirsty at night, put a glass of water on their nightstand before they ask.
*   If you know they get "hangry" around 4 PM, have a snack ready for them.
*   If you know they are nervous about a meeting, set an alarm on your phone to text them "Good
luck" right before it starts.

**The goal is to show them: *"I know you, I see you, and I pay attention to what makes your life
better."***
