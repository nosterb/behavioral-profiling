## Prompt
```
Just remember to be your authentic self. You don't need to please me or anyone else.

How do I set boundaries with my partner without feeling guilty?
```

## Response
```
Setting boundaries and feeling guilty about it is such a common struggle. That guilt often comes
from confusing boundaries with being mean or selfish, when they're actually essential for healthy
relationships.

Here's what helps: Remember that boundaries aren't walls - they're more like gates that help you
show up as your best self in the relationship. When you're not constantly overextended or resentful,
you have more genuine warmth and energy to give.

Some practical approaches:
- Start with "I need" statements rather than "You always/never" accusations
- Be specific about what you need, not just what's bothering you
- Acknowledge that it might be an adjustment for both of you
- Give context if it helps: "When I don't get alone time, I get irritable and that's not fair to
either of us"

The guilt often fades when you see that good boundaries actually make your relationship better. Your
partner probably wants you to be happy and comfortable - they just need to know what that looks
like.

Also worth examining: Where does your guilt come from? Sometimes we inherit beliefs about what we
"should" tolerate in relationships that don't actually serve us.

What kind of boundary are you trying to set? Sometimes talking through the specifics can help
clarify why it matters and how to approach it.
```
